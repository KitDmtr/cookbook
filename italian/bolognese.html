Bolognese sauce

A rich and flavorful Italian meat sauce

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 carrot, finely chopped
  • 1 celery stalk, finely chopped (mirepoix)
  • 1 pound ground beef (80/20 lean-to-fat ratio)
  • 4 ounces pancetta, finely diced (optional)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • (14.5 oz) can crushed tomatoes
  • (28 oz) can diced tomatoes, undrained
  • 1 cup dry red wine (such as Chianti or Merlot)
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat. Add the onion, carrot, and celery (mirepoix) and cook for 5-7 minutes, or until softened and translucent.
  2. (Optional) Add the pancetta to the pot and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, or until crispy. Remove the pancetta from the pot with a slotted spoon and set aside.
  3. Increase the heat to medium-high and add the ground beef. Cook, breaking it up with a spoon, until browned on all sides.
  4. Stir in the garlic and cook for an additional 30 seconds, or until fragrant.
  5. Pour in the crushed tomatoes, diced tomatoes, red wine, beef broth, tomato paste, oregano, thyme, and bay leaf. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat to low and cook partially covered for 1-2 hours, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens and flavors meld.
  6. (Optional) Add the cooked pancetta back to the sauce and simmer for an additional 10 minutes.
  7. Remove the bay leaf before serving. Serve the bolognese sauce over cooked pasta, such as tagliatelle, spaghetti, or lasagna. Enjoy!
