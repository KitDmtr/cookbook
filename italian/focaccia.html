Focaccia Bread

A delicious and easy Italian flatbread

Ingredients

  • 3 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 ¼ teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 ½ teaspoons salt, plus extra for sprinkling
  • 1 ½ cups warm water (105°F to 115°F)
  • ¼ cup extra virgin olive oil, plus more for drizzling
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary (optional)
  • 2 cloves garlic, halved (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, yeast, and sugar. Make a well in the center.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the warm water and salt. Pour the wet ingredients into the well of the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. The dough will be sticky.
  3. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for 5-7 minutes, or until smooth and elastic. Add a little more flour if the dough is too sticky.
  4. Place the dough in a large, oiled bowl. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let the dough rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours, or until doubled in size.

  5. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
  6. Lightly oil a baking sheet. Transfer the dough to the prepared baking sheet and spread it out into a 12-inch x 16-inch rectangle, using your fingertips to gently press and dimple the dough.
  7. Drizzle the top of the dough with olive oil. Using your fingertips, make indentations all over the dough. Season generously with salt.
  8. (Optional) Top the focaccia with fresh rosemary sprigs and garlic halves, pressing them gently into the dough.
  9. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the focaccia is golden brown and crusty on top.
  10. Let the focaccia cool slightly on a wire rack before slicing and serving. Enjoy!**
