Ramen

A flavorful and satisfying noodle soup

Ingredients

For the Chicken Broth:

  • 8 cups chicken bones (backs, necks, or a combination)
  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces (optional)
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 2 carrots, roughly chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, roughly chopped
  • 1 inch fresh ginger, peeled and sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, smashed
  • (Optional) 1 whole scallion, charred
  • Water

For the Tare (Seasoning Sauce):

  • ½ cup soy sauce
  • ¼ cup mirin (sweet rice wine)
  • ¼ cup sake (Japanese rice wine)
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, grated

For the Assembly:

  • 12 ounces fresh ramen noodles (or dried ramen noodles, cooked according to package instructions)
  • Sliced cooked pork belly (chashu)
  • Marinated soft-boiled eggs (ajitama)
  • Menma (fermented bamboo shoots) - optional
  • Nori seaweed sheets, cut into small squares - optional
  • Scallions, thinly sliced - optional
  • Black sesame seeds - optional

Instructions

    For the Chicken Broth:

  1. In a large pot, combine the chicken bones (and whole chicken, if using), onion, carrots, celery, ginger, garlic, and charred scallion (optional) with enough water to cover by 2 inches. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for at least 2 hours, or until the broth is flavorful and has reduced by half. Skim off any scum that rises to the surface during simmering.
  2. Strain the broth through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean pot. Discard the solids.

    3. For the Tare (Seasoning Sauce):

  3. In a small saucepan, combine the soy sauce, mirin, sake, brown sugar, fish sauce, garlic, and ginger. Bring to a simmer over medium heat and cook for 5 minutes, or until the sugar dissolves and the sauce thickens slightly. Remove from heat and let cool.

    4. Assembly:

  4. Bring the chicken broth back to a simmer in a pot.
  5. Cook the ramen noodles according to package instructions (if using dried noodles). If using fresh noodles, cook them in boiling water for 1-2 minutes, or until tender.
  6. Divide the cooked noodles among serving bowls.
  7. Ladle the hot broth over the noodles in each bowl.
  8. Top each bowl with sliced chashu, a marinated soft-boiled egg (ajitama), menma (optional), nori seaweed (optional), scallions (optional), and black sesame seeds (optional).
  9. Serve immediately and enjoy!
